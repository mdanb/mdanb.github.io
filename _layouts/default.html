Mohamad Daniel Bairakdar

I am a computational research associate at the Tsankov Lab in the Department of Genetics and Genomic Sciences at the Icahn School of Medicine at Mount Sinai.

I am broadly interested in using statistical/machine learning, and statistical tools more generally, to solve pressing biomedical problems. I'm especially passionate about contributing to aging research and research on the three main age-related diseases: Cancer, Heart Disease, and Dementia.

I have a B.S.E degree in Data Science and a minor in Mathematics (equivalent to a full degree in Mathematics in terms of math coursework) from the University of Michigan Ann Arbor. At Michigan, I mainly worked in Ambuj Tewari's and Matthias Truttmann's labs.
